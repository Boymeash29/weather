FLASH FLOOD WARNING
Expires: April 4, 2025, 4:30 PM EDT
Areas Affected:
Riverside County, San Bernardino County
Issued:
April 4, 2025, 11:30 AM EDT
NWS Office:
San Diego, CA
Source:
Doppler Radar

Hazard

Flash flooding caused by excessive rainfall is expected.

Source

Doppler radar indicated thunderstorms producing heavy rain across the warned area. Between 1 and 2 inches of rain have fallen. Additional rainfall amounts of 1 to 2 inches are possible in the warned area. Flash flooding is ongoing or expected to begin shortly.

Impact

Flash flooding of small creeks and streams, urban areas, highways, streets and underpasses as well as other poor drainage and low-lying areas.

Recommended Actions

Turn around, don't drown when encountering flooded roads. Most flood deaths occur in vehicles. Be aware of your surroundings and do not drive on flooded roads.

Source: National Weather Service
SEVERE THUNDERSTORM WATCH #127
Expires: April 4, 2025, 8:00 PM EDT
Areas Affected:
Jackson County, Madison County, Lincoln County
Issued:
April 4, 2025, 12:00 PM EDT
NWS Office:
Memphis, TN

Primary Threats Include

Scattered large hail and isolated very large hail events to 2 inches in diameter possible. Scattered damaging wind gusts to 70 mph possible. A tornado or two possible.

Meteorological Summary

A severe storm system will move through central portions of the Mississippi Valley this afternoon and evening, interacting with an unstable airmass. Thunderstorms are likely to develop during the afternoon hours and could become severe.

Recommended Actions

Remember, a Severe Thunderstorm Watch means conditions are favorable for severe thunderstorms in and close to the watch area. Persons in these areas should be on the lookout for threatening weather conditions and listen for later statements and possible warnings.

Source: National Weather Service
